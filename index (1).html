Case study · Featured work

AskDoc+ — Designing trust
into AI health conversations

The problem: People were Googling symptoms at 2am and coming away more scared than informed. Existing health apps either gave generic information or pushed users straight to emergency care. Nobody was helping them understand — calmly, clearly, accurately.

My role: I designed AskDoc+ end-to-end as founder and sole product designer. From first user interview to App Store submission. Every screen, every interaction, every design decision was mine to make and defend.

The constraint that made this hard: This is healthcare. Every word on screen could affect a real decision about someone's health. The AI had to feel helpful without overstepping. Confident without being reckless. I had to design trust into every touchpoint — from the first message to the moment a user decides whether to call a doctor.

The core design challenge
How do you make AI feel safe in a context where the stakes are genuinely high? Every design decision — how the AI phrases an answer, how urgency is communicated, what happens when the AI is uncertain — had real consequences for real people. I had to design a system that was warm enough to feel approachable, clear enough to be understood, and honest enough to be trusted.
💬
Conversational UX
Designed the full conversation flow — from opening prompt to follow-up questions. Every turn was mapped to reduce anxiety and increase comprehension. Users feel guided, not interrogated.
🔍
AI Explainability
Designed confidence framing, source attribution, and grounded disclaimers that tell users what the AI knows and what it does not. Uncertainty is visible, not hidden — that is a trust feature.
🛡️
Trust & Safety UX
Urgency indicators that escalate appropriately — calm for routine concerns, clear and direct for anything that warrants professional care. Designed to de-escalate anxiety, not amplify it.
🎨
Design System
Built a complete system in Figma — components, tokens, interaction states — enabling rapid iteration from prototype to App Store without losing visual consistency across every screen.
♿
Accessible Design
Designed with WCAG principles throughout. Healthcare users include people who are anxious, unwell, or elderly — accessibility is not optional here, it is core to the product doing its job.
